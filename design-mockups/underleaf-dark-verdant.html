A more verdant
Underleaf Dark.

Three studies in pushing the non-black greens warmer, leafier, and lightly floral — while keeping the slate-blue chassis the reader already trusts.

Curator's note.
The current dark theme reads competent but cool — a Material green floating in slate. What we want is something that grows: a forest read at night, where the deep blues stay (the bark, the sky) and the accents shift to chlorophyll — moss, leaf, the suggestion of a petal. The blacks remain inky and stable so the eye can rest; the not-blacks bloom.

Reading the
specimens.

① Moss

Lowest visual noise. Best for long writing sessions. Loses some signal — comments and keywords are closer in chroma. Pick if you want the editor to "disappear."

② Verdance

Best contrast budget across token types. The pink-floral math literal is a single bright accent without overwhelming. Pick if you read a lot of math.

③ Wildflower

The "garden in low light" option. Brand stays leaf-green, type names + accents bloom petal-pink. Pick if you want personality without giving up legibility.

Three roads from the same trailhead. — specimen sheet ends
