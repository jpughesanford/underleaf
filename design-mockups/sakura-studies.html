Hanami studies · light theme № 05

Six ways to read
a bloom.

Earlier drafts leaned all-pink or all-peach. Five of these push the green back into view — toolbar bough, tea-garden inversion, pond reflection, weathered moss, woodblock graphic. The sixth steps out of the green entirely: pale spring sky behind the bloom. Pick one.

Curator's note.
A real sakura scene is never one colour. The eye reads a triad — blossom pink, grass green, and the dark line of a branch. The problem with v.iv was treating the chrome as a single pink wash; the syntax did the rest of the lifting alone. The first five studies each find a different home for the green; the sixth swaps the green for the spring sky the branches reach into.
— six paths,
one bloom.

Choosing
between
them.

① Hanami

Closest to the brief: pink ground, green bough in the toolbar. Pick if you want a small surprise without changing the reading surface.

② Matcha

Full inversion. Green is the body; pink appears only at the references. Pick if you read more than you reference.

③ Mizu

Cool, watery, contemplative. Petals reflected in a pond. Pick for long evenings; the celadon is restful.

④ Petal Fall

Quiet, weathered. Aged cherry over dusty moss. Pick if the other four feel too saturated.

⑤ Edo

Graphic, declarative. Saturated woodblock pink on rice paper with carved green. Pick if you want personality at the cost of subtlety.

⑥ Harukaze

The view looking up. Periwinkle sky, coral bloom, ivory sunlight. Pick if you want spring without committing to green — or if the other five all feel too earthbound.

Six readings of the same tree. — specimen sheet ends
