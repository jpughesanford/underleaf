Premise.
A light theme should not be white. Paper is warmer than that — and the things written on it ought to feel like ink, not pixels. We start from the petal, work toward the leaf, and let the bark live only at the toolbar.

ii.

How it composes.

Reading notes.

One bright petal.

The brand (sakura coral) is the only highly-saturated color in the entire theme. It earns its attention because nothing else competes — the focus ring, the cursor, the active-file dot all share it.

Plum, not black.

Primary text is #2b1218 — a deep aubergine. Against the warm paper, true black would feel printed-too-hard; plum sits with the warmth instead of shouting over it.

Leaves as labels.

Sage green is reserved for label content — what \label{…}, \cite{…}, \ref{…} point at. Labels are the "fixed points" of a document. Leaves are the fixed points of a tree.
